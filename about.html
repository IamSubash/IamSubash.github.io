About Subash:

Subash Pandey is a Ph.D. student at the Department of Chemistry, Michigan Technological University. He is working as a Graduate Research Assistant in Perrine Research Lab.

He is broadly interested in surface reactions, corrosion, surface catalysis, and electrochemical reduction reactions. In his recent research, he is studying reactions at the gas/solid interface and liquid/solid interface.

Subash received a B.S. Degree in Chemistry in 2016 and an M.Sc. Degree in Physical Chemistry in 2020 from Tribhuvan University, Nepal. He did his M.S. level dissertation to develop highly efficient supercapacitor electrode materials using Ce-doped polyaniline with magnetite nanocomposites having higher power density, energy density, and cycle life.

He also has several years of experience as a teaching assistant in high school and university settings.
